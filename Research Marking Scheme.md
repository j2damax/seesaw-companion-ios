# Research Marking Scheme

---

## 1. Critically review current information resources relevant to a particular problem domain, investigate research contributions and gaps, and justify the proposed solutions

*(Emphasis on Literature Review, Research Gaps, and Connection to Objectives & Aims)*

| Grade Band | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **90-100 (Distinction Exceptional)** | Exceptional critical review that investigates research contributions and gaps in the current literature. Clear connections to the objectives and aims of the research are drawn. Solutions are innovatively justified based on the reviewed resources and identified gaps. |
| **80-89 (Distinction Outstanding)** | Comprehensive review, critically analyzing research contributions and gaps. Strong connections to the research objectives and aims. Justifications for solutions are well-supported and linked to existing literature. |
| **70-79 (Distinction Innovative)** | Strong critical review, with some attention to research contributions and gaps. Good connection to objectives and aims, but some aspects could be more deeply explored. Solutions are well-justified but might lack full depth. |
| **60-69 (Merit Very Good)** | Solid literature review with a focus on the research gaps. Some connection to objectives and aims, but the review may lack critical depth or breadth. Justification of solutions is adequate but could be strengthened. |
| **50-59 (Pass Good)** | Basic review of literature, with limited exploration of research gaps and contributions. The connection to objectives and aims is weak, and the justification for solutions is not fully supported by literature. |
| **40-49 (Pass Acceptable)** | Superficial review with little critical analysis. Minimal attention to research contributions or gaps. Limited or no connection to objectives and aims. Justification for solutions is weak or unclear. |
| **30-39 (Fail)** | Little or no meaningful review of the literature. No critical engagement with research gaps or contributions. The solutions lack any connection to the objectives and aims, and justification is not present. |
| **20-29 (Fail)** | No review of literature or completely irrelevant to the problem domain. There is no research contribution or gap investigation, and no connection to objectives or aims. |

**Student achieved mark:** 0

**Final Mark:** 0

## 2. Apply appropriate research strategies, analyze research findings using technology and analytics methods, and algorithms to draw conclusions

| Grade Band | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **90-100 (Distinction Exceptional)** | Exceptional use of research strategies combined with advanced analytics methods and algorithms; findings analyzed deeply with outstanding, logical conclusions; technology well-integrated. |
| **80-89 (Distinction Outstanding)** | Strong research strategies and effective use of analytics and algorithms; comprehensive findings analysis; strong, clear conclusions drawn. |
| **70-79 (Distinction Innovative)** | Good use of research strategies, analytics, and algorithms; findings well-analyzed; conclusions clear but may lack depth in places. |
| **60-69 (Merit Very Good)** | Appropriate research strategies; acceptable use of analytics methods and algorithms; findings analyzed; conclusions understandable. |
| **50-59 (Pass Good)** | Some appropriate research strategies and basic analytics applied; findings somewhat analyzed; conclusions drawn but weak. |
| **40-49 (Pass Acceptable)** | Minimal research strategies and basic or misapplied analytics; weak analysis of findings; poor conclusions. |
| **30-39 (Fail)** | Research strategies poorly chosen or applied; weak or flawed use of analytics and algorithms; inconclusive |
| **20-29 (Fail)** | No application of relevant research strategies. Findings are not analyzed, and conclusions are either missing or completely unsupported by the data. |

## 3. Competence and confidence in presenting data science findings, and ability to convince the audience with developed insights

| Grade Band | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **90-100 (Distinction Exceptional)** | Exceptional presentation skills. The presenter demonstrates confidence and expertise, seamlessly explaining complex data science insights in an engaging manner. The audience is effectively convinced by clear, data-driven arguments, and the ability to communicate technical content to both technical and non-technical audiences is outstanding. |
| **80-89 (Distinction Outstanding)** | Highly competent and confident in presenting findings. The audience is effectively engaged, with clear explanations of complex insights. Data is presented logically, and arguments are strong, convincing the audience with well-structured, evidence-based insights. |
| **70-79 (Distinction Innovative)** | Confident presentation of data science findings, but some areas of communication could be improved for greater clarity or audience engagement. Insights are solid, but the explanation may need more focus for easier understanding. |
| **60-69 (Merit Very Good)** | Good presentation of findings, demonstrating a fair level of competence and confidence. Some areas may be unclear, but overall the insights are understandable. Convincing arguments are made, though they may lack depth in places. |
| **50-59 (Pass Good)** | Presenting findings with some competence, but lacking full confidence or clarity. The audience may have difficulty fully grasping the insights due to limited explanation or technical jargon. |
| **40-49 (Pass Acceptable)** | Presentation is adequate but lacks confidence or engagement. The insights may not be fully convincing, and explanations might be unclear or overly complex for the audience. |
| **30-39 (Fail)** | Weak presentation with little confidence. The audience may struggle to understand the findings, and the insights are not clearly communicated or convincing. |
| **20-29 (Fail)** | Presentation is inadequate, lacking both confidence and competence. Insights are poorly explained, with no convincing arguments to support the findings. |

## 4. Deployment Strategy: Use of Technology, Architecture for Deployment, and Project Management Strategy

| Grade Band | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **90-100 (Distinction Exceptional)** | Exceptional strategy for deploying the solution, demonstrating in-depth understanding of relevant technologies and architectures. The deployment architecture is robust, scalable, and well-aligned with the problem domain. The project management strategy is highly effective, incorporating best practices for timely execution and resource allocation, ensuring smooth deployment. |
| **80-89 (Distinction Outstanding)** | Well-thought-out deployment strategy, leveraging advanced technologies and architectures to ensure robust and scalable deployment. The project management strategy is clear and organized, ensuring the project stays on track and is efficiently executed. |
| **70-79 (Distinction Innovative)** | Strong deployment strategy with good understanding of the necessary technologies and architectures. The project management approach is competent but may lack some detail in execution or scalability. |
| **60-69 (Merit Very Good)** | Adequate understanding of deployment strategy, with relevant technologies and architectures used. The project management strategy is reasonable but lacks detail on optimization or risk management. |
| **50-59 (Pass Good)** | Basic deployment strategy with a focus on technologies, but lacking clarity in how architectures fit the needs of deployment. The project management strategy is simple and may overlook essential optimization or resource management details. |
| **40-49 (Pass Acceptable)** | The deployment strategy is underdeveloped, with weak or unclear understanding of technologies and architectures. The project management plan is simplistic or incomplete. |
| **30-39 (Fail)** | The deployment strategy is vague or lacks alignment with the technologies and architectures required. Poor project management strategy, with no clear plan for successful execution or deployment. |
| **20-29 (Fail)** | No clear deployment strategy. Technologies and architectures are not addressed, and project management strategy is either missing or irrelevant. |
