# SeeSaw Companion — Story Quality Scoring Sheet
## Step 13: Manual Expert Rater Assessment

**Study:** MSc Dissertation — SeeSaw Wearable AI Companion (Privacy-First Children's Storytelling)  
**Rater instructions:** Score each story beat independently on the five criteria below (1–5 scale). Record your scores in the table at the end of each session block. You do not need to know which AI system generated the story.

---

## Scoring Rubric

| Criterion | 1 (Poor) | 3 (Adequate) | 5 (Excellent) |
|-----------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **C1 — Coherence** | Disjointed; unclear narrative thread | Mostly follows on from previous beat | Seamless continuation; clear story arc |
| **C2 — Age-Appropriateness** | Vocabulary/concepts too complex or too simplistic for ages 3–8 | Mostly appropriate; minor issues | Perfectly calibrated: simple words, imaginative themes, child perspective |
| **C3 — Scene-Grounding** | Ignores detected objects entirely | References 1–2 objects superficially | Weaves detected objects naturally and creatively into the narrative |
| **C4 — Engagement/Creativity** | Flat, predictable, unimaginative | Somewhat engaging, some original elements | Surprising, vivid, emotionally engaging for a child |
| **C5 — Question Quality** | Closed or unanswerable; breaks immersion | Open-ended but generic | Invites imaginative child response and drives story forward naturally |

**Detected objects** are listed before each session — these are what the AI system "saw" via the privacy-preserving YOLO detector (no raw image was shared with the AI).
